Hiragana & Katakana

What are kana?

Japanese uses two basic sound alphabets (kana): hiragana and katakana. Each symbol represents a simple sound like ka, to, or mi.

Hiragana is used for native Japanese words and grammar. Katakana is used mainly for foreign words, names, and emphasis.

Basic vowel sounds

Start by remembering these five core sounds. They are the same in hiragana and katakana:

  • a (あ / ア) — like “a” in “father”
  • i (い / イ) — like “ee” in “see”
  • u (う / ウ) — like “oo” in “food” (short)
  • e (え / エ) — like “e” in “bed”
  • o (お / オ) — like “o” in “go” (short)
